# One-Day Meal Plan for Marathon Preparation (Vegetarian Carbohydrate Loading)

**Athlete Profile:** 178cm, 68kg
**Goal:** Marathon preparation with carbohydrate loading for endurance

* **Calorie Target:** Approximately 3,200 calories
* **Macronutrient Breakdown:** Protein (110g), Fat (80g), Carbs (480g)

---

## **Meal Plan:**

* **Breakfast (7:00 AM):** Oatmeal (100g dry weight) with banana (150g), raisins (40g), and almond milk (250ml). Side of whole wheat toast (60g) with peanut butter (20g).
    * Protein: 15g, Fat: 12g, Carbs: 95g

* **Mid-Morning Snack (10:00 AM):** Energy bar (70g) and orange juice (300ml).
    * Protein: 8g, Fat: 8g, Carbs: 65g

* **Lunch (1:00 PM):** Lentil and vegetable pasta (200g cooked pasta + 150g lentil-based sauce), mixed green salad (100g) with olive oil dressing (15ml), and whole grain bread (80g).
    * Protein: 28g, Fat: 15g, Carbs: 110g

* **Afternoon Snack (4:00 PM):** Banana (150g) with Greek yogurt (200g) and granola (40g).
    * Protein: 18g, Fat: 8g, Carbs: 85g

* **Dinner (7:00 PM):** Chickpea curry (250g chickpeas + 150g vegetables in coconut milk sauce) served over white rice (180g cooked), with naan bread (60g).
    * Protein: 28g, Fat: 20g, Carbs: 110g

* **Evening Snack (9:30 PM):** Rice cakes (50g) with honey (25g) and almonds (25g).
    * Protein: 7g, Fat: 9g, Carbs: 35g

---

**Key Features:**
- High carbohydrate content (480g) supports glycogen storage for marathon endurance
- Adequate protein (110g) supports muscle recovery and repair
- Emphasis on plant-based proteins: legumes, yogurt, nuts, and grains
- Budget-friendly options using dried legumes and grains
- Timing spreads carbs throughout the day for optimal energy availability
