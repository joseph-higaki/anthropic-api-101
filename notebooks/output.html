# One-Day Meal Plan for Marathon Preparation

Here is a one-day meal plan for an athlete preparing for marathon competition with carbohydrate loading:

* **Calorie Target:** Approximately 3,500 calories
* **Macronutrient Breakdown:** Protein (140g), Fat (95g), Carbs (520g)

## Meal Plan:

* **Breakfast (7:00 AM):** Oatmeal (100g dry weight) with banana (150g), honey (30g), and whole milk (240g). Side of whole wheat toast (60g) with almond butter (20g).
    * Protein: 18g, Fat: 12g, Carbs: 110g

* **Mid-Morning Snack (10:00 AM):** Energy bar (65g) and sports drink (500ml).
    * Protein: 10g, Fat: 8g, Carbs: 75g

* **Lunch (1:00 PM):** Grilled chicken breast (150g), brown rice (150g cooked), steamed sweet potato (200g), and mixed vegetables (100g) with olive oil (15ml).
    * Protein: 45g, Fat: 12g, Carbs: 95g

* **Afternoon Snack (3:30 PM):** Bagel (85g) with cream cheese (30g) and sliced turkey (50g). Orange juice (250ml).
    * Protein: 18g, Fat: 10g, Carbs: 90g

* **Pre-Dinner Snack (5:00 PM):** Greek yogurt (200g) with granola (40g) and blueberries (80g).
    * Protein: 20g, Fat: 8g, Carbs: 75g

* **Dinner (7:00 PM):** Lean ground beef (150g) with pasta (200g cooked), marinara sauce (150g), and garlic bread (60g with 15ml olive oil).
    * Protein: 35g, Fat: 20g, Carbs: 95g

* **Evening Snack (9:30 PM):** White bread sandwich (80g) with jam (40g) and honey (20g). Coconut water (250ml).
    * Protein: 4g, Fat: 5g, Carbs: 80g

---

**Hydration Strategy:** Drink water consistently throughout the day (3-4 liters minimum) and include sports drinks during and after training sessions for electrolyte replacement and sustained energy.

This meal plan emphasizes complex and simple carbohydrates for glycogen loading, adequate protein for muscle recovery, and strategic timing to support marathon training performance.
